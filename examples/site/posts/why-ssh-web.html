Why SSH-Web?

May 5, 2026

The problem with HTTPS

HTTPS bolts encryption onto a plaintext protocol via a trust hierarchy of certificate authorities. SSH-Web starts from SSH's encrypted, authenticated foundation and builds web semantics on top.

Structural privacy

On an SSH-Web site, third parties never see the client. External resources (fonts, CDN assets) are server-proxied through an allowlist. No tracking pixels, no cross-origin leaks, no ambient authority.

Identity via keypair

No passwords, no OAuth flows, no third-party identity providers. Your ed25519 keypair is your identity. The server knows your public key; you prove you hold the private key. That's it.
